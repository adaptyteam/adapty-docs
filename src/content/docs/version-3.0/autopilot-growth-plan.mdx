After you complete [the analysis](autopilot-analysis), Adapty presents your growth plan — a list of **actionable improvement hypotheses**. Each item suggests a new price point or design improvement.

Open a hypothesis to [try it out with an A/B test](autopilot-execute-plan).

Each placement has its own growth plan. As market conditions change, you can re-run the analysis to refresh suggestions. Past runs stay in the version history.

<ZoomImage id="growth-plan.webp" width="700px" alt="growth plan overview" />

## Hypotheses

Switch between tabs at the top of the growth plan to filter hypotheses by type:

<ZoomImage id="hypothesis-tabs.webp" width="700px" alt="hypothesis tabs" />

- **Top priority** includes the highest-impact hypotheses that deserve your attention. When no hypothesis qualifies, this tab is hidden.
- **All** shows every hypothesis from your active plan.
- **Pricing** hypotheses explore new price points or trial configurations. Each is based on a specific recommendation from the paywall diagnostic or market insights report.
- **Visual** hypotheses are design improvement suggestions. They may involve changes to copy, layout, or other visual elements.
- [**Geo-pricing**](#geo-pricing-hypotheses) hypotheses test country-specific price adjustments.
- [**Archived**](#archive-a-hypothesis) hypotheses are suggestions you removed from your active plan. Restore them anytime.

You can [add your own hypothesis](#add-your-own-hypothesis), or [archive](#archive-a-hypothesis) any you don't want to test.

Test these hypotheses one at a time, in any order. Geo-pricing tests are the exception — their audiences don't overlap, so they can run in parallel.

### Geo-pricing hypotheses

:::important
One-time purchases are not eligible for regional price optimization.
:::

<ZoomImage id="autopilot-geo-rounds-overview.webp" width="400px" float="left" />

Open the **Geo-pricing** tab to see the list of geo-pricing recommendations. Each recommendation targets one country with a single price change and runs as a separate A/B test.

Adapty detects countries that require pricing adjustments and provides data-driven recommendations validated by the [Adapty Pricing Index](https://uploads.adapty.io/adapty_pricing_index.pdf).

<br />

#### How Adapty makes geo-pricing suggestions

- Pricing recommendations are based on App Store data. The resulting A/B test can run on both App Store and Google Play.
- The price change percentage is the same across all subscription durations.
- All prices are rounded to the nearest App Store price tier.
- Prices appear in local currency (for example, EUR or GBP) if Adapty has transaction data for that country. When no local data is available, prices are shown in USD.

### Hypothesis status badges

1. **Lightning bolt** — indicates top-priority suggestions.
2. **Product sync status** — appears when a product action is required to launch the A/B test.
        - **Draft** — the product setup is incomplete (Adapty-side)
        - **Action required** — the product setup is incomplete (Store-side)
        - **Pending...** — Adapty is waiting for the store to complete the review or the initial sync.
        - **Approved** — the product has been approved by the store and is ready for testing.
        - **Rejected** — the store rejected the product.
        - **Not connected** — the product isn't linked to the store yet.
3. **A/B test status** — appears when you launch the A/B test:
        - **Draft test** — the test is drafted but not running yet.
        - **Running** — the test is live.
        - **Completed** — the test has finished.
        - **Archived test** — the test was archived without conclusion.

## Get new AI-generated hypotheses

<ZoomImage id="autopilot-toolbar.webp" width="300px" float="right"/>

After each test, Adapty automatically updates your hypotheses based on the outcome.

To pull in fresh competitor pricing, conversion benchmarks, and category trends, click **Update** <Inline id="refresh.svg" alt="Refresh" /> in the growth plan header — or **Update Analysis** on the Autopilot homepage. Click **Get New Ideas** when prompted.

Adapty opens the analysis wizard with your placement pre-selected. Repeat the paywall analysis and competitor research, and Adapty identifies new hypotheses from the result.

Pick the ones you want to add, or click **Add All To Plan** <Inline id="plus.svg" alt="Plus" /> to accept everything. Newly added hypotheses appear at the top of the list. Your existing AI-generated hypotheses, custom hypotheses, and any A/B tests in progress are unaffected.

<ZoomImage id="autopilot-update.webp" width="700px" />

If you exit an update before finishing, you can **resume it** to continue, or **discard it** to start anew.

<ZoomImage id="unfinished-analysis.webp" width="700px" />

## Add your own hypothesis

<ZoomImage id="autopilot-add-hypothesis.webp" width="400px" float="right"/>

Click **Add Hypothesis** <Inline id="plus.svg" alt="Plus" /> to create your own pricing or visual suggestion.

Fill the form: title, description, and hypothesis type (**Monetization** or **Visual**).

- Select the metrics you hope to improve from the dropdown menu.
- Monetization hypotheses also require selecting test products.

## Archive a hypothesis

<ZoomImage id="skip-hypothesis.webp" width="300px" float="right" />

To archive a hypothesis, click the <Inline id="close.svg" alt="Close" /> button, then Skip to confirm. You can optionally explain why — it helps Adapty refine future suggestions.

The hypothesis moves to the **Archived** tab.

To restore an archived hypothesis to your active plan, click **Restore** on the card.

<ZoomImage id="autopilot-archived-tab.webp" width="700px" />

## Revisit and reuse old hypotheses

<ZoomImage id="analysis-version-history.webp" width="300px" float="right" />

To browse suggestions generated by past analyses, click **Clock** <Inline id="clock.svg" alt="Clock" /> in the growth plan header. The **Version history** modal lists every past run for the placement — date, paywall, and how many of its hypotheses you accepted at the time.

Click a past run to see the hypotheses it produced. You can add any of them to your active plan with **Add to Plan** <Inline id="plus.svg" alt="Plus" /> — useful for revisiting suggestions you didn't pick up the first time.
