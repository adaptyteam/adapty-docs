Adapty Mail sends from your own domain — not from a shared address — so your sender reputation stays under your control. You set this up once and it applies to all your campaigns.

## Requirements

:::important
- Submit your root domain (for example, `yourapp.com`), not a subdomain.
- The domain must have active NS records.
- One domain per Adapty project; a domain cannot be shared across projects.
:::

## Add your domain

1. In Adapty Mail, go to **Settings → Domain**.
2. Enter your root domain and click **Continue**.
3. Review the DNS records preview and click **Confirm**.

Adapty automatically creates two sending subdomains: `mail.yourapp.com` and `email.yourapp.com`. Each subdomain gets its own SES identity with independent authentication.

## Add DNS records

Add 10 DNS records to your domain registrar (5 per subdomain) before starting verification.

:::note
DNS changes can take up to 48 hours to propagate, though most registrars update within minutes.
:::

For each subdomain, add:

| Type | Purpose | Count | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| CNAME | DKIM | 3 | Cryptographic signatures that prove the email wasn't altered in transit |
| MX | Mail-From | 1 | Handles bounces |
| TXT | SPF (Mail-From) | 1 | Authorizes Adapty to send on your behalf |

Additionally, add one record on your root domain:

| Type | Purpose | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| TXT | DMARC | Recommended for enhanced deliverability; optional |

## Verify your domain

After adding all DNS records:

1. Return to **Settings → Domain** in Adapty Mail.
2. Click **Verify**.

Adapty checks your DNS records automatically every 5 minutes. Verification typically completes within **10–30 minutes**.

:::important
The verification window expires after **7 days**. If it lapses, your DNS records remain in place — return to **Settings → Domain** and re-enter your domain to start a new verification window.
:::

## Domain warm-up

When you first send from a new domain, email providers like Gmail and Yahoo have no reputation data for it. Email providers like Gmail and Yahoo treat new domains as high-risk and may route high-volume sends to spam.

Adapty Mail manages warm-up automatically — no configuration required.

### How warm-up works

Sending volume increases gradually across 14 tiers as your deliverability metrics stay healthy:

| Tier | Daily limit |
|---|---|
| 1 | 200 |
| 2 | 400 |
| 3 | 800 |
| 4 | 1,500 |
| 5 | 2,500 |
| 6 | 4,000 |
| 7 | 6,000 |
| 8 | 8,000 |
| 9 | 10,000 |
| 10 | 13,000 |
| 11 | 16,000 |
| 12 | 20,000 |
| 13 | 25,000 |
| 14 | 30,000 |

Your domain starts at Tier 1 and advances automatically when:

- Bounce rates stay below safe thresholds
- Spam complaint rates are minimal
- Delivery rates remain strong

If metrics degrade, tier advancement pauses or reverses until the domain's reputation recovers.

You can monitor your current tier and daily sending limit in **Settings → Domain**.

### Impact on launch by audience size

| Audience size | Effect at launch |
|---|---|
| Under 200 users | Full audience reached on day one |
| 200–2,000 users | Delivery spreads over several days |
| 2,000+ users | Delivery spreads over 1–2 weeks |

:::tip
Launch your first campaign as soon as DNS verification completes. The sooner you start sending, the sooner you advance through tiers and reach full daily capacity.
:::

## Troubleshooting

| Issue | Solution |
|---|---|
| Verification stuck | Check that DNS records match exactly — no trailing dots, correct CNAME targets |
| "Domain already registered" | The domain is in use by another Adapty project. Use a different domain |
| Emails going to spam | Confirm your DMARC record is published. New domains need warm-up time |
| Bounce rate high | Verify that your audience list contains valid, opted-in addresses |
