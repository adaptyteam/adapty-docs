:::tip
Growth Autopilot works best with apps that already generate revenue. If Adapty can analyze your financial metrics, its suggestions are more likely to be effective.
:::

Growth Autopilot is a data-driven paywall optimization tool that uses advanced analytics to generate an actionable growth plan for your app.

* Autopilot [analyzes your paywall's content and design](#paywall-diagnostic), highlights things that work, and detects **opportunities for conversion improvement**.
* It pulls country-specific **market data** for your app category, [compares your app](#market-insights) to its competitors, and [detects pricing mismatches](#geo-pricing-rounds) in different markets.
* After the analysis is complete, Autopilot presents an [actionable growth plan](#your-growth-plan). Each round is an A/B test to try out a new approach. Based on our estimates, completing the entire test cycle could increase your revenue **by up to 80%**.

:::important
- The analysis is based on data from the Apple App Store.
- The analysis focuses only on subscription products. Consumable in-app purchases are not included.
- Competitor research reports are country-specific. This allows us to understand the differences across markets.
:::

## Quickstart

1. Open the **A/B tests** page and click the [Adapty autopilot](https://app.adapty.io/ab-tests/analysis/start) button. Click **Get your Growth Plan** to open the paywall selection screen.

        <ZoomImage id="pricing-analysis.webp" width="700px" />

2. [Select a Placement and Paywall](#select-and-confirm-the-paywall). Confirm that the products displayed are correct. Upload a screenshot and click **Confirm & Analyze**.

        <ZoomImage id="autopilot-select-placement.webp" width="600px" />

3. Adapty [analyzes](#paywall-diagnostic) your paywall's design, content, and revenue metrics. Review the report to find out what's working and what needs improvement.

        <ZoomImage id="autopilot-paywall-diagnostic.webp" width="600px" />

4. [Select up to 5 competitors](#select-competitors) for an in-depth comparison. Click **Generate report**.

        <ZoomImage id="competitors.webp" width="700px" />

5. Read the [market insights](#market-insights) report to learn about:
    * Geographic revenue distribution
    * Competitor pricing (country-specific)
    * Conversion funnel benchmarks (country-specific, market average)

        <ZoomImage id="industry-benchmarks.webp" width="700px" />

6. Open the [growth plan](#your-growth-plan) and review Adapty's data-driven suggestions. Each round is an A/B test that targets a single pricing or design change. Add your own hypotheses to expand the plan.

        <ZoomImage id="growth-plan.webp" width="700px" />

7. [Run the tests](#run-the-tests) round by round. Create new products, duplicate your paywall with the suggested changes, and launch the A/B test. The winner advances to the next round.

        <ZoomImage id="hypothesis.webp" width="700px" />

8. To keep your growth plan relevant, regularly [update](#update-your-analysis) the analysis. As market conditions change, suggestions may become outdated.

## Select a paywall

1. On the **Paywall Diagnostic** page, select a **Placement** and **Paywall** from the dropdowns. Adapty pre-selects the highest-revenue placement and its top paywall. To analyze a different paywall, change the placement first.

        <ZoomImage id="autopilot-select-placement.webp" width="600px" />

2. Upload a screenshot. The Growth Autopilot needs a screenshot to analyze your paywall design and content.
3. Review the paywall's active products. Product cards on the right display each product's subscription duration, price, and trial period.
4. Click **Confirm & Analyze** to proceed. Adapty analyzes your paywall and displays the diagnostic report.

## Paywall Diagnostic

After you select a paywall and upload a screenshot, Adapty analyzes your paywall for established design patterns, highlighting both good choices and improvement opportunities.

<ZoomImage id="autopilot-paywall-diagnostic.webp" width="600px" />

### What's working well

This section highlights your use of established patterns that maximize conversion. For example: a visible savings badge, a prominent user review section, or clear subscription breakdowns.

### What to fix on your paywall

Adapty groups its recommendations into two categories:

- **Benchmarked recommendations**: Data-driven suggestions based on top-performing apps in your category. Each recommendation includes a benchmark stat (for example, "Used by 72% of top-performing Education apps") and a description of what to change.
- **Visual analysis recommendations**: AI-generated suggestions based on your paywall screenshot. These include: copy improvements, layout changes, and more.

:::tip
Your [growth plan](#your-growth-plan) will include A/B test rounds based on the benchmarked recommendations. You can add visual analysis suggestions to the plan manually.
:::

Click **Get Market Insights** to proceed.

## Select competitors

The rest of the analysis compares your app's performance to your competitors. You need to select up to 5 competitors for the comparison.

Adapty will pick 5 automatically, and suggest 5 more. You can manually add apps with an App Store link. For better results, select apps with higher MRR than yours.

<ZoomImage id="competitors.webp" width="700px" />

Click **Generate report** to confirm the list, and wait for the analysis to finish.

## Your growth plan

Adapty presents your growth plan as a series of **rounds**. Each round proposes a single change to your pricing or paywall design. You can run this change as an **A/B test** to see if it improves your metrics.

<ZoomImage id="growth-plan.webp" width="700px" alt="growth plan overview" />

The Growth plan page displays Adapty's suggestions based on the data it gathered during the previous steps. Switch between different tabs on the screen to filter suggestions by type:

- **Pricing hypotheses** explore new price points or trial configurations. Each round is based on a specific recommendation from the paywall diagnostic or market insights report.
- **Visual hypotheses** are design improvement suggestions. They may involve changes to copy, layout, or other visual elements.
- [Geo-pricing hypotheses](#geo-pricing-rounds) test country-specific price adjustments. They require [extra setup](#how-to-run-a-geo-pricing-round). 

You can [add your own](#expand-the-plan) pricing and visual hypotheses. If you don't like a hypothesis, you can [delete it](#remove-a-round). 

The tests based on these suggestions can run in any order, but only one at a time. Since the audiences of geo-pricing rounds do not overlap, these tests can run in parallel with one another.

### View suggestion details

<ZoomImage id="hypothesis.webp" width="700px" alt="hypothesis view" />

Click a hypothesis entry to learn more about the recommendation. The round card includes the following sections:

- **Hypothesis**: A data-driven explanation of how your paywall deviates from established patterns for conversion optimization.
- **How to test your idea**:
    * Changes you need to make to your paywall.
    * Suggested A/B test setup.
- **How to interpret your results**: The metrics you need to track to determine the success of the test.

Click the **Exit** button to go back to the growth plan overview.

:::important
One-time purchases are not eligible for regional price optimization.
:::

Adapty detects countries that require pricing adjustments and provides data-driven recommendations validated by the [Adapty Pricing Index](https://uploads.adapty.io/adapty_pricing_index.pdf).

#### How Adapty makes geo-pricing suggestions

- Pricing recommendations are based on App Store data. The resulting A/B test can run on both App Store and Google Play.
- The price change percentage is the same across all subscription durations.
- All prices are rounded to the nearest App Store price tier.
- Prices appear in local currency (for example, EUR or GBP) if Adapty has transaction data for that country. When no local data is available, prices are shown in USD.

#### How to run a geo-pricing round

:::warning
If you run other pricing rounds alongside geo-pricing tests, watch for audience overlap.

If a non-segmented pricing round runs at the same time as a geo-pricing round, users from the target country may enter both tests. To avoid conflicting results, make sure your regular pricing rounds exclude countries that have active geo-pricing tests.
:::

<ZoomImage id="geopricing-test-idea.webp" width="700px" />


1. Create new products for the test

    Each A/B test compares your current product against a test variant with the adjusted price. If your Adapty setup doesn't already include an appropriate product, the round will display a **Create Product** button.

    * Create one new product for each subscription duration.
    * Reuse the same product across countries: set the country-specific prices in App Store Connect.

2. Create a new paywall for the new products

    Duplicate your current paywall and assign new products to the copy. If your app supports localization, make sure the copy is translated for the target country.

3. Create segments

    For each country, create a [segment](segments) that filters users by **Country**.

4. Launch A/B test

    The round pre-fills a test draft with these settings:

    - **Variant A**: Your current paywall with the original version of the product
    - **Variant B**: The duplicated paywall with the modified version of the product at the new country-specific price
    - **Segment**: The target country
    - **Traffic split**: 50/50

    Review the draft and click **Run A/B test** to start the test.

    Geo-pricing tests may take longer to reach statistical significance because of country-level segmentation. Consider running them for at least 4 weeks.

### Expand the plan

Autopilot allows you to expand your growth plan with new rounds. Click the "Add Hypothesis" button to add a new hypothesis.

<ZoomImage id="autopilot-add-hypothesis.webp" width="400px" />

Fill the form: add a title, description, and select the type of round (**Monetization** or **Visual**).

- Select the metrics you hope to improve from the dropdown menu.
- When adding a monetization round, you need to specify which products the test will involve.

### Remove a round

If you don't like a particular suggestion, you can skip it. To remove a round from your growth plan, open a hypothesis and click the **Skip the round** button. This action is irreversible. You can later add a new hypothesis manually.

<ZoomImage id="skip-autopilot-round.webp" width="700px" />

### Get new suggestions and update the growth plan

Click the **Update** button to refresh your analysis with the latest market data. The system runs your analysis again and updates the recommendations.

<ZoomImage id="autopilot-toolbar.webp" width="300px" />

### Restore an older version of the growth plan

Click the **Clock** button to view the version history and return to an earlier version of the analysis.

<ZoomImage id="analysis-version-history.webp" width="300px" />

## Run the tests

You can run the tests in any order, but you need to run them **one at a time**. Once a test is over, advance the winning strategy to the next round. The winner of the final round represents the most efficient strategy.

Based on our estimates, completing the entire test cycle could increase your revenue **by up to 80%**.

:::important
Each suggestion includes the minimum duration for the A/B test. Follow these recommendations to get the most accurate data before you begin with the next stage. You will need to stop the A/B test manually.
:::

To start the first stage:

1. Follow the instructions in the hypothesis view to create new products if necessary.
2. Duplicate your existing paywall to modify it. To generate a new design, you can use the [Adapty Paywall Builder](adapty-paywall-builder)'s built-in AI capabilities.
3. Launch an A/B test to compare how the new paywall performs against the current paywall.

When you start a test, the suggestion card will reflect its active status. Click the card to view test details:
